Technology

To sense and to serve.

Understanding technology

Can technology observe and identify the people, products and places of the physical world in real-time? Can technology understand the context of their physical and digital interactions?

We believe so, and we developed our technology to do just that.

Sense and serve

There are billions of radio-identifiable "things". Our sensors observe these on a human scale, relaying their data and real-time location to our Pareto platform.

The people, products and places represented by these "things" exist digitally on the Internet. Our Pareto platform connects their physical presence with their digital representation, relaying this contexual information to third-party platforms in real-time.

Our sensors

Infrastructure to observe the real world on a human scale

Our sensors are effectively invisible buttons. Each button is triggered by the presence of a radio-identifiable device. Pressing invisible buttons is equivalent to browsing physical webpages.

The reelceiver

Our sensors are called reelceivers. Reelceivers listen for radio-identifiable devices of a given protocol, such as Bluetooth Low Energy. Detection range is on the order of 10m.

Reelceivers simply convert radio packets into serial packets which they send via the reel.

The reel

Reelceivers connect to power and network via the reel. The reel is a daisy chain of reelceivers using standard network cables, connected at one end to a hub.

The reel's flexible configuration can be used to extend coverage over hundreds of metres and to position reelceivers for optimal location granularity.

The reel is a plug-and-play, install-and-forget infrastructure.
